---
layout: recipe
yield: 8
time: 4
tags:
- bread

ingredients:
- 360g AP flour (3 cups)
- 11g salt (1.75 tsp)
- 1 tsp instant yeast
- 1 tsp sugar
- 284g warm water (1.25 cups)
- 7.5 Tbsp light olive oil
- 2/3 cup light brown sugar, packed
- 4 Tbsp butter, softened
- 2 Tbsp cinnamon
- 3/4 cup confectioners' sugar, sifted
- 1.5 Tbsp milk
- 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
- pinch of salt
directions:
- Whisk flour, salt, yeast, and sugar. Add water and 1.5 Tbsp olive oil and mix until homogeneous
- Cover and rest dough for 15 minutes
- Combine brown sugar, butter, and cinnamon into a smooth paste, cover and set aside
- Perform two sets of dough folds with 15 minutes rest after each fold
- Perform two more sets of dough folds with 15 minutes rest after each fold. Sprinkle 1/4 of cinnamon mixture over the dough before both folds
- Cover and let rise for 1 hour until doubled and puffy
- Spray the bottom of your pan with oil, line with parchment, then spray the parchment. Add 1 Tbsp olive oil and spread around bottom evenly
- Gently move dough to pan, drizzle 1 Tbsp olive oil over dough
- Let rise for 1-1.5 hours until jiggly, close to end preheat oven to 450F
- Dimple dough with oiled fingers, top with remaining cinnamon mixture, and dimple again to push cinnamon down a bit
- Drizzle with 1 Tbsp olive oil and bake for 15-20 minutes until deep golden brown. Can tent with foil if browning too quickly
- Transfer to wire rack to cool
- Make glaze by mixing confectioners' sugar with milk and vanilla extract. Drizzle over focaccia and allow to set for several minutes
- Serve slightly warm or at room temperature

notes:
- Best day its made, but can wrap loosely in foil at keep at room temperature
- Don't use glass pans because they won't crisp up the bread properly. Can use a metal pan or cast iron skillet
- Can make dough ahead of time by transfering to refrigerator after the 1 hour bulk rise before transfering to the pan, for 8-24 hours. Transfer the cold dough to a pan and let rise for 2-3 hours so it can warm to room temperature before dimpling
