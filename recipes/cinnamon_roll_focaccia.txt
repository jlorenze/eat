Cinnamon Roll Focaccia

Yield: 8

Time (hours): 4

Keywords: bread, dessert

Recommended Sides:

Ingredients:
360g AP flour (3 cups)
11g salt (1.75 tsp)
1 tsp instant yeast
1 tsp sugar
284g warm water (1.25 cups)
7.5 Tbsp light olive oil
2/3 cup light brown sugar, packed
4 Tbsp butter, softened
2 Tbsp cinnamon
3/4 cup confectioners' sugar, sifted
1.5 Tbsp milk
1/2 tsp vanilla extract
pinch of salt

Make:
1. Whisk flour, salt, yeast, and sugar. Add water and 1.5 Tbsp olive oil and mix until homogeneous.
2. Cover and rest dough for 15 minutes.
3. Combine brown sugar, butter, and cinnamon into a smooth paste, cover and set aside.
4. Perform two sets of dough folds with 15 minutes rest after each fold.
5. Perform two more sets of dough folds with 15 minutes rest after each fold. Sprinkle 1/4 of cinnamon mixture over the dough before both folds.
6. Cover and let rise for 1 hour until doubled and puffy.
7. Spray the bottom of your pan with oil, line with parchment, then spray the parchment. Add 1 Tbsp olive oil and spread around bottom evenly.
8. Gently move dough to pan, drizzle 1 Tbsp olive oil over dough.
9. Let rise for 1-1.5 hours until jiggly, close to end preheat oven to 450F.
10. Dimple dough with oiled fingers, top with remaining cinnamon mixture, and dimple again to push cinnamon down a bit.
11. Drizzle with 1 Tbsp olive oil and bake for 15-20 minutes until deep golden brown. Can tent with foil if browning too quickly.
12. Transfer to wire rack to cool.
13. Make glaze by mixing confectioners' sugar with milk and vanilla extract. Drizzle over focaccia and allow to set for several minutes.
14. Serve slightly warm or at room temperature.

Notes:
1. Best day its made, but can wrap loosely in foil at keep at room temperature.
2. Don't use glass pans because they won't crisp up the bread properly. Can use a metal pan or cast iron skillet.
3. Can make dough ahead of time by transfering to refrigerator after the 1 hour bulk rise before transfering to the pan, for 8-24 hours. Transfer the cold dough to a pan and let rise for 2-3 hours so it can warm to room temperature before dimpling.
