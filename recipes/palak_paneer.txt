Palak Paneer

Yield: 4-6

Time (hours): 0.75

Keywords: indian

Recommended Sides: paneer, rice, naan

Ingredients:
16 oz fresh spinach
2 green chilies, roughly chopped 
2 cloves garlic, roughly chopped
1 inch ginger, roughly chopped
4 Tbsp oil or ghee or unsalted butter
1 tsp cumin seeds
1 onion, finely chopped
8 cloves garlic, finely chopped
2 medium sized tomatoes, chopped
1/2 tsp turmeric powder
1/4 tsp red chili powder
3/4 tsp garam masala powder
8 oz paneer or tofu, cubed
salt

Make:
1. Rinse spinach well. If stems are not tender (e.g. stringy), discard the stems.
2. Boil ~6 cups water and blanch spinach for 1 minute. <blanch>
3. After spinach is cooled, transfer to blender with green chilies, 2 cloves roughly chopped garlic, and ginger. Blend into a smooth puree.
4. Heat oil, ghee, or butter (if using butter make sure to not let it brown). Add cumin seeds and let them sputter for about a minute.
5. Add onion and saute until golden, then add remaining (finely chopped) garlic and saute for an additional 30 seconds to 1 minute.
6. Add tomatoes and saute until softened. Add turmeric powder and red chili powder and mix well.
7. Add spinach puree and water and stir to reach desired consistency (~3/4 cup water).
8. Simmer until spinach is cooked (~7 minutes), and season with salt.
9. Add garam masala powder, plus additional to taste, and stire.
10. Add paneer and simmer until paneer becomes soft (~1 minute).
