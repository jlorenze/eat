Falafel

Yield: 6

Time (hours): 1

Keywords: mediterranean

Recommended Sides: tahini sauce, tzatziki, cucumbers, hummus

Ingredients:
2 cups dried chickpeas
1/2 tsp baking soda
1 cup fresh parsley
3/4 cup fresh cilantro
1/2 cup fresh dill
1 small onion, quartered
7-8 garlic cloves
1 Tbsp salt
1 Tbsp black pepper
1 Tbsp cumin
1 Tbsp coriander
1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
1 tsp baking powder
canola, peanut, or other frying oil

Make:
1. One day in advance, soak dried chickpeas in water with baking soda for 18 hours or longer.
2. Rinse and drain chickpeas, then dry completely.
3. Blend/process chickpeas, herbs, onion, garlic, and spices into a coarse meal texture.
4. Refrigerate mixture for at least 1 hour to make shaping easier.
5. While heating oil on med-high heat to 375F, mix baking powder into falafel mixture.
6. Form falafel mixture into balls or 1/2-inch thick patties, and fry for 3-5 minutes or until crispy and browned.
7. Drain falafel in colander or paper towel-lined plate.
8. Serve hot.

Notes:
1. Canned or cooked chickpeas won't work.
2. Can freeze uncooked falafel patties, or refrigerate for a couple of days before cooking. Falafel can be cooked from frozen.
3. Can also coat in oil and bake at 350F for 15-20 min, turning halfway.
