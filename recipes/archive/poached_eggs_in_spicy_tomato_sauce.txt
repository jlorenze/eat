Poached Eggs in Spicy Tomato Sauce

Yield: 4

Time (hours): 0.75

Keywords: breakfast, meat

Recommended Sides: bacon, fruit

Ingredients:
2 tomatos, cored and halved
2 onions, chopped
3 tbsp olive oil
2 yellow bell peppers, chopped
4 garlic cloves, minced
2 tsp tomato paste
salt and pepper
1 tsp ground cumin
1 tsp ground turmeric
1/8 tsp cayenne pepper (or less)
1 1/2 cups jarred roasted red peppers, chopped
1/2 cup water
2 bay leaves
1/3 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
8 large eggs

Make:
1. Heat broiler and move rack to 6 inches.
2. Toss tomatoes and onions with 1 tbsp oil and put on aluminum foil lined backing sheet. (put tomato cut side down)
3. Broil until veggies softened and skin charred. (~8 min)
4. Process tomato and onion until coarse.
5. Saute bell peppers in remaining 2 tbsp oil until soft and lightly browned. Stir in garlic, tomato paste, and spices and cook until paste darkens. (~ 3 min)
6. Stir in processed tomato mixture, red peppers, water, and bay leaves. Simmer and cook until sauce is thickened. (~ 10 min)
7. Discard bay leaves, add cilantro. Process 2 cups sauce until smooth and return to skillet. Bring back to simmer.
8. Off heat, make 4 indentations. Crack 2 eggs into each and season with salt and pepper. Cover and cook over medium low heat until eggs are cooked to satisfaction.
